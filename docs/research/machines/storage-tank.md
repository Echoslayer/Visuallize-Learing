# Storage Tank / Feed Tank Research

## Overview
Feed tanks serve as the initial supply reservoir in industrial fluid processing pipelines. They store raw materials before the material is pumped and pressurized into the system. As the starting node of the process, they define the pipeline's input constraints and buffer capacity.

## Component Breakdown (Supply Chain Perspective)
1. **Main Body (Shell)**: Large cylindrical container, typically made of stainless steel or specialized alloy to handle chemical or sanitary requirements.
2. **Roof / Head**: Often conical or domed for pressure containment, structural integrity, and rain/debris drainage.
3. **Supports (Legs)**: Structural legs supporting the tank to elevate it from the skid base, allowing gravity feed or bottom clearance for piping.
4. **Piping/Connections**: Outlet pipes located at the bottom to feed fluid directly into the pump skid.
